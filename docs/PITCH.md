# NeuroSync — 7-Minute Jury Pitch (slide-by-slide + script)

> Format: **~7 min present + ~3 min Q&A** to a senior leadership jury.
> Golden rule the jury stated: **problem first, solution second.** No tech jargon
> up front. Tell a human story, then *show* it working.
> Timings are a guide — rehearse to land at **6:45** so you never get cut off.
> Pair this with `DEMO-SCRIPT.md` (the live demo) and `QA-PREP.md` (the Q&A).

**Speaking order suggestion:** one person narrates Slides 1–5 (story + problem),
a second person drives the live demo (Slides 6–7), the first closes (Slides 8–10).
Keep it human — **AI must not present on your behalf** (jury rule).

---

## SLIDE 1 — Title (0:10)
**On screen:** `NeuroSync` · *Work, in sync with how you think.* · team names · one calm hero image.
**Say:** "Hi, we're team NeuroSync. Before we show you anything we built, we want to tell you about one ordinary Monday morning."
**Design:** lots of white space, one line of text, high contrast. Set the calm tone immediately.

---

## SLIDE 2 — The hook: meet Aanya (0:45)
**On screen:** a photo/illustration of a person at a laptop; the words "38 emails. 4 chat threads. 6 tickets. One hidden deadline."
**Say:**
> "Meet Aanya. She's a talented analyst — the kind of person you want on your team. Aanya has ADHD and dyslexia. It's 9am Monday. Her inbox has 38 unread emails, four chat threads, six tickets. One email from her manager hides three tasks and a Thursday deadline in the third paragraph. Aanya reads it four times. The panic rises. She opens a ticket, a notification pulls her away, and an hour later — she still hasn't *started*. By Thursday, the deadline is a surprise."

**Say (turn):** "Nothing here is about Aanya's ability. It's about a workday built for a brain that isn't hers."
**Design:** no logos, no UI yet. Just the human moment.

---

## SLIDE 3 — The problem (1:00)
**On screen (one sentence):** *"Turning workplace text into started, finished work carries a hidden 'cognitive tax' — and for people with ADHD and dyslexia, it's far higher."*
**Say:**
> "Modern work is a firehose of text — emails, chats, tickets. The tasks and deadlines are buried *inside* the prose. Mainstream tools assume you can read fast, hold it all in your head, and just start. For the people we're talking about, every one of those steps costs extra energy. The result: missed deadlines, burnout, and constant *masking* to look like everyone else."

**Say (business framing):** "This isn't a niche wellbeing issue — it's wasted talent and avoidable attrition."
**Design:** the single sentence, big. One small supporting stat (Slide 4 carries the numbers).

---

## SLIDE 4 — Who, and how big (0:45)
**On screen (3 numbers):**
- **15–20%** of people are neurodivergent
- **~1 in 2** people with ADHD also have dyslexia → *one connected problem*
- **Accenture's own research:** inclusion leaders saw **28% higher revenue, 2× net income, 30% higher profit margins**

**Say:**
> "Why these two together? Because ADHD and dyslexia *co-occur about half the time* — so we're not solving two problems, we're solving one connected one: the cost of turning text into action. And the business case isn't ours — it's Accenture's. Accenture's own research shows companies leading on disability inclusion outperform their peers. NeuroSync is how we operationalise that for the neurodivergent talent already inside our walls."
**Design:** three big numbers, source line in small text under each (credibility).

---

## SLIDE 5 — What NeuroSync is + 3 USPs (1:00)
**On screen:** *"An inclusive work companion that turns chaos into clarity — adapted to how your brain works."* + three icons:
1. **Adaptive output** — same message, rendered for your brain
2. **Understanding → doing** — we help you *start*, not just understand
3. **Inclusion-first & enterprise-native** — calm by default, on your data

**Say:**
> "NeuroSync does three things nobody else does together. **One** — adaptive output: the same email becomes time-boxed steps for an ADHD brain, or short, plain, dyslexia-friendly text for a different reader. Copilot gives everyone the *identical* summary. **Two** — we close the gap from understanding to *doing*: realistic steps, a focus mode, and a reset when you're overwhelmed. **Three** — it's inclusion-first and built to live on enterprise data, not a public chatbot."
**Say (bridge to demo):** "Instead of more slides — let us show you."

---

## SLIDE 6 — LIVE DEMO (2:30)  → follow `DEMO-SCRIPT.md`
**On screen:** the running app (switch to the browser).
**Beats (full narration in DEMO-SCRIPT.md):**
1. Paste Aanya's messy email into the **Summarizer** → real AI returns summary, **action items**, the **hidden Thursday deadline**, tone, and a **dyslexia-friendly rewrite**. Toggle **Dyslexia mode**.
2. Send it to the **Task Planner** → AI breaks it into **micro-steps with time estimates**.
3. Hit **Focus Mode** → everything fades but the *next* step.
4. Click **"Feeling overwhelmed?"** → the calm breathing reset.
**Say (closing the demo):** "Same email. But now it's understood, broken down, started — and Aanya never had to mask or ask for help."
**Design:** font size up in the app; have a **pre-recorded 60s clip as backup** in case of network/AI failure.

**Say:**
> "Generic AI summarises. NeuroSync *adapts to the person*, helps them *act*, supports them in the moment, and respects enterprise data. That combination is the moat."
**Design:** checkmarks, not paragraphs.

---

## SLIDE 8 — Responsible & inclusive by design (0:30)
**On screen:** *No diagnosis · Human-in-the-loop · Sourced nudges · Inclusive by default*
**Say:**
> "We're careful because this community is sensitive. NeuroSync never diagnoses or labels anyone — support is a *choice*, not an assumption. The AI suggests; the human decides. Our wellbeing nudges are based on recognised techniques, not invented advice. And the calm, accessible design is on for everyone."
**Design:** four short pillars.

---

## SLIDE 9 — Feasibility, scale & roadmap (0:20)

**Say:** "What you just saw is real and running on Azure with Google Gemini. It scales as an enterprise layer, and the adaptive-output engine is built to extend to more languages and needs."

---

## SLIDE 10 — Close + the ask (0:20)
**On screen:** `NeuroSync` · *Work, in sync with how you think.* · "Let neurodivergent talent do its best work."
**Say:**
