# NeuroSync — User Research Kit

> The reviewers' biggest validation gap: *"you're building for neurodivergent
> users but haven't talked to them."* This kit lets you run **5–10 short, ethical
> interviews** and turn them into quotable evidence for the pitch. You don't need
> many — even **5 conversations** changes your story from *"we assume"* to
> *"users told us."*

> **Now live in the product:** NeuroSync includes an opt-in personalization questionnaire (no diagnosis). Its preferences — simpler language, smaller steps, gentler reminders — are exactly what to validate here. Use interviews to confirm these help and to discover new ones.

---

## Ground rules (this community is sensitive — read first)
- **Voluntary & anonymous.** No one has to participate; no names on slides.
- **Never require a diagnosis.** People can share as much or as little as they want. You are researching *workday friction*, not anyone's medical status.
- **No "fixing" language.** You're learning how work *could fit them better* — not what's "wrong."
- **Consent before recording.** Prefer written notes over audio unless they agree.
- **Right to skip/stop** any question, any time.
- **Store notes privately** (no public repos / no client data). Aggregate before sharing.

## Recruiting: the ask to HR / I&D (copy-paste)
> *Subject: 15 minutes to shape an inclusion tool (hackathon finalist)*
>
> Hi [name], we're a finalist in the [hackathon] building NeuroSync — a tool to
> reduce the daily "cognitive tax" of emails/tickets for employees with ADHD and
> dyslexia. We'd love **5–10 volunteers** for an **optional, anonymous 10-minute
> chat** about workday friction. No diagnosis or disclosure required — anyone who
> finds dense work communication draining is welcome. Could you help us reach
> the [ERG / neurodiversity network], or share this opt-in link? Thank you!

**Also try:** your neurodiversity ERG, accessibility champions, or a simple opt-in form. Aim for a mix of roles. 5–8 is plenty for a hackathon.

## Consent script (say this at the start)
> "Thanks for your time. This is anonymous and totally voluntary — skip anything
> you like, and we can stop whenever. There are no wrong answers; we're learning
> how work communication could fit people better. You don't need to share any
> diagnosis. Is it okay if I take written notes? Any questions before we start?"

## Interview guide (~10 minutes)
Keep it conversational. **Ask, then listen** — don't pitch your idea early, and don't lead.

### A. Warm-up (1 min)
1. "Roughly what does a typical workday look like for you — how much is email, chat, tickets, docs?"

### B. Discover the problem (4–5 min) — *the gold is here*
2. "Walk me through the last time a long or messy message at work was hard to deal with. What happened?"
3. "When a task lands in your inbox, what's the hardest part — understanding it, prioritising it, or **starting** it?"
4. "How do you cope today? Any tricks, tools, or workarounds?"
5. "How do reminders and notifications feel — helpful, or stressful? Why?"
6. "Has reading load or focus ever affected a deadline or your energy at the end of a day?"

> Probes: "Can you give an example?" · "What did that feel like?" · "What did you do next?"

### C. React to the concept (3 min) — *only now show it*
Show the live app: an email → summary + action items + hidden deadline + dyslexia-friendly rewrite. Then show the **personalization** — that the same email can be rendered for *their* preferences (shorter text / smaller steps).
7. "What's your honest first reaction?"
8. "Which part — if any — would actually help on a Monday? Which wouldn't?"
9. "Would having the output adapt to how *you* read and work (shorter text, smaller steps) make a difference?" *(this validates the core USP)*
10. "What would make you hesitant to use this at work?" *(listen for stigma/privacy)*
11. "If you had a magic wand for your inbox, what would it do?"

### D. Close (30s)
12. "Anything we didn't ask that we should have?" Thank them; offer to share what you learn.

### Don'ts
- ❌ Don't ask "Would you use this?" (everyone says yes). Ask about *past behaviour* instead.
- ❌ Don't ask leading questions ("Wouldn't it be great if…").
- ❌ Don't ask anyone to disclose a diagnosis.

## Synthesis template (turn notes into evidence)
After each chat, jot 3 lines. After all of them, fill this in:

```
Participants: N people, roles: ___ (kept anonymous)

Top pain points (count how many raised each):
1. ________________________  (mentioned by __/N)
2. ________________________  (mentioned by __/N)
3. ________________________  (mentioned by __/N)

Strongest verbatim quotes (anonymised):
- "____________________________________"
- "____________________________________"

What resonated most about NeuroSync: ____________
What did NOT resonate / concerns: ____________
Surprises that should change the product: ____________
```

### How it appears in the pitch
- **One anonymised quote** on the problem slide (Slide 3): e.g. *"By the time I've re-read the email, I've lost the morning." — Analyst, anonymised.*
- **One stat from your own research** on Slide 4: *"X of Y people we interviewed said **starting** a task is harder than understanding it."*
- A Q&A-ready line: *"We didn't just assume — we asked, and here's what they told us."*
